The Challenge

Lead generation via quote form.

"A construction company's website has one job above all else: get the client to reach out. Every section needed to build trust, and the form needed to make that next step feel easy."

The main challenge was designing a quote request form that was detailed enough to be useful for the BuildCo team, but simple enough that potential clients wouldn't abandon it halfway through.

Form validation using Zod ensured data quality on the backend, while the UI was designed to guide users step by step — project type, details, contact info — without feeling like a survey.

The rest of the site was built to earn trust before asking for the click — services, process, credentials, and social proof all leading toward that single conversion point.

Process

How I approached it.

01

Conversion-First Planning

Started by identifying the single most important action on the site: requesting a quote. Every section was then planned backwards from that goal — what does a visitor need to see and believe before they're ready to fill out a form?

02

Form UX Design

Designed the quote form to feel structured but not overwhelming. Grouped fields logically — project type first, then details, then contact. Wrote clear placeholder text and labels so users always knew what was being asked and why.

03

Schema Validation with Zod

Implemented Zod schema validation for the form — ensuring all submitted data met the required format before processing. This meant fewer incomplete submissions and cleaner data for the BuildCo team to work with.

04

Services Layout & Responsive Build

Built the services showcase to clearly communicate BuildCo's capabilities without overwhelming the reader. Refined all breakpoints for mobile and tablet — making sure the form in particular was easy to complete on a phone.
